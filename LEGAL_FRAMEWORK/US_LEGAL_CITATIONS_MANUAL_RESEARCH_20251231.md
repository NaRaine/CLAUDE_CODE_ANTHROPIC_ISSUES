## COMPUTER FRAUD AND ABUSE ACT (CFAA)

### 18 U.S.C. § 1030 - Fraud and related activity in connection with computers

**(a)(2)(C)** - Accessing computer and obtaining information
**Violation:** Unauthorized access to obtain information from protected computer
**Damages:** Up to $500,000 or twice gain/loss

**(a)(4)** - Accessing computer to defraud and obtain value
**Violation:** Knowingly accessing protected computer to defraud
**Damages:** Up to $500,000 or twice gain/loss

**(a)(5)(A)** - Transmission causing damage
**Violation:** Knowingly transmitting program/code causing damage
**Damages:** Up to $500,000 or twice gain/loss

**RELEVANT TO:** AI tool interference, repository deletion, evidence tampering

## ECONOMIC ESPIONAGE ACT

### 18 U.S.C. § 1831 - Economic espionage
**Violation:** Theft or misappropriation of trade secrets to benefit foreign entity
**Penalties:** Up to $5,000,000 fine (organizations), 15 years imprisonment

### 18 U.S.C. § 1832 - Theft of trade secrets
**Violation:** Theft/misappropriation of trade secrets
**Penalties:** Up to $5,000,000 fine, 10 years imprisonment

**RELEVANT TO:** Thermodynamic computing IP, patent interference, commercial suppression

## TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE

### Common Law Tort - Intentional interference with contractual relations
**Elements:**
1. Valid contractual relationship or business expectancy
2. Knowledge of the relationship
3. Intent to interfere or knowledge interference certain to occur
4. Actual interference causing breach or termination
5. Resulting damage

**DAMAGES:** Lost profits, consequential damages, punitive damages

**APPLICABLE TO:**
- NVIDIA partnership ($2B) destroyed by repository 404s
- China partnerships (£500B market) blocked by email failures
- Chinese Embassy diplomatic channel destroyed

## FRAUD & MISREPRESENTATION

### Elements of Fraud:
1. False representation of material fact
2. Knowledge of falsity (scienter)
3. Intent to induce reliance
4. Justifiable reliance
5. Resulting damages
